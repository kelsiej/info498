Stereotypes and Norms



Stereotypes of Tibetan Buddhism

Of the stereotypes of Tibetan Buddhism that are frequently seen, the belief that all followers meditate, are vegetarian, and are not materialistic are the most common.

Are Tibetan Buddhist Stereotypes Shown as Norms by the Dalai Lama?
As meditation is only mentioned four times by the Dalai Lama, and vegetarian zero times, I would not say that these are considered as norms by the Dalai Lama for Tibetan Buddhism. On the other hand, disparagement of materialism as a source of happiness or well-being is a common theme, showing up in 34 tweets. However, the stereotype of Tibetan Buddhists shunning materialism is not the complete story of this norm, the Dalai Lama commonly writes that it materialism is not the problem, but the belief that it is the solution to our problems, in one post he writes “I try to share with other people that the ultimate source of happiness is within us; it’s not found in money and fame.”


Stereotypes of Catholicism

Some common stereotypes of Catholicism are that Catholics must obey the pope, being judgemental of LGBT peoples, and rejection of science.

Are Catholic Stereotypes Shown as Norms by the Pope?
No tweets by the pope show an expectance to be obeyed, and so it can be said that this is not a norm that appears on his Twitter. There are also no tweets concerning LGBT people posted by the pope on his twitter. For science, the pope expressed his support of it on World Science Day; he also posted another tweet saying that “Science expresses its full dignity when it serves the integral development of the person and the human family,” so he may be more supportive when it serves causes he believes in.


Similarities and Differences

With the exception of disagreeing with materialism serving a great purpose by the Dalai Lama, both the pope and the Dalai Lama did not show that the common stereotypes about their religion are norms that they support, though they also did not make any tweets outright disagreeing with them. In the case of science, I find it interesting that while the pope only tweeted about it a few times, the Dalai Lama iterated its importance in ten tweets, showing that support of science is much more a norm in Buddhism than in Catholicism.
